About

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Energy, Environmental & Chemical Engineering at Washington University in St. Louis. My research focuses on reactions of critical elements and inorganic contaminants at iron oxide water interfaces.

I combine environmental geochemistry, electrochemistry, and materials characterization to understand how adsorption, redox, and solid transformation processes govern contaminant mobility. I am particularly interested in flow through electrocoagulation systems and iron mineral transformations relevant to drinking water treatment and groundwater quality.

Research

Current themes

I build mechanistic pictures that connect mineral scale reactions to treatment performance and long term water quality outcomes.

Flow through iron electrocoagulation

How reactor configuration, hydrodynamics, and iron dosing shape arsenic and selenium removal across complex water chemistries.

Redox active iron minerals

Formation and transformation of green rust, magnetite, and related phases, and their roles in reducing selenium and chromium in oxic and anoxic environments.

From batch experiments to long term operation

Linking short term kinetic experiments, solid phase characterization, and long term performance to anticipate treatment outcomes beyond a single water matrix.
