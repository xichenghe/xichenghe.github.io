About

About me

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Energy, Environmental & Chemical Engineering at Washington University in St. Louis. My research focuses on electrochemical and geochemical processes that control the behavior of inorganic contaminants in natural and engineered water systems, with an emphasis on electrocoagulation, interfacial redox reactions, and solid phase transformations.

Research

Research interests

My research aims to understand how adsorption, redox reactions, and mineral transformation control the fate of contaminants such as arsenic, selenium, chromium, uranium, and rare earth elements. I combine bench scale flow through experiments with electrochemical methods and advanced solid characterization to build mechanistic models that can inform treatment design and risk assessment.
